# Content-addressed preflight cache (single-slot, cross-iteration)

The iteration-scoped preflight gate (`ensure_preflight` + `Deps.preflight_verdict`) collapses into a single `PreflightCache` object owned by the orchestrator and threaded through every iteration's `Deps`. The cache holds **one slot**: the most recently observed safe SHA's verdict (`PreflightReady | PreflightAFK | PreflightHITL`). Every save-SHA-consuming caller invokes `await deps.preflight_cache.get_safe_sha(deps)`; the method internally serialises via `asyncio.Lock`, runs `git pull --ff-only` on every call, reads HEAD, returns the cached verdict iff the slot's SHA matches HEAD, otherwise runs preflight in an ephemeral `transient_worktree("preflight-sandbox")` and updates the slot. The cache is process-scoped and constructed once in `orchestrator.run()` *outside* the iteration loop. The `skip_preflight` flag on `RunRequest` and the per-agent `runner.preflight()` call inside `agent_runner.run()` are removed; the Merger's preflight collapses into the same `get_safe_sha()` call site that improve and planning use. `PreflightFailure` (the exception subclass introduced in ADR 0013) is removed — it has no remaining raise site.

The trigger was issue #639: iteration 1's improve agent hit the usage limit during the Scan phase; the orchestrator switched accounts and started iteration 2; iteration 2's first act was to re-run the Preflight Agent over the same improve-sandbox at the same HEAD SHA. Two redundancies surfaced. First, `Deps.preflight_verdict` is iteration-scoped: `Deps` is reconstructed every iteration in the orchestrator loop, so the cached verdict is discarded even when nothing about the validated state has changed. Second, the gate's "is this SHA safe" predicate is structurally content-addressable — preflight runs against a tree pinned at a SHA, the answer depends only on the tree's content and the configured check list — but the existing cache was scoped to the iteration container rather than to the SHA itself. A SHA-keyed cache that survives across iteration boundaries cleanly captures both wins (no re-preflight on usage-limit retry, no re-preflight when HEAD didn't move) without weakening any existing safety property.

A second observation drove the scope expansion. After auditing call sites, every agent invocation in production passes `skip_preflight=True` except the Merger. The single live consumer of the per-agent preflight inside `agent_runner.run()` is the merge-time check that runs against the post-clean-merge HEAD inside the merge-sandbox container. That check is not "is this SHA safe to base new work on" — it is "did the clean merges I just landed leave the baseline broken." But the predicate it evaluates ("does this tree's content pass `PREFLIGHT_CHECKS`") is the same predicate the gate evaluates, just over a tree whose SHA originated from local merge work rather than upstream. Pulling the merger's check into the same `get_safe_sha()` mechanism unifies the two paths under one cache and one failure shape, with the merge-time soft-skip semantics preserved at the call site.

## Considered Options

- **Status quo: iteration-scoped `Deps.preflight_verdict`.** Rejected: the failure mode in #639. The cache loses information at iteration boundaries that is structurally still valid — every usage-limit retry, every iteration that doesn't advance HEAD, repeats work whose result has not changed. Cross-iteration correctness is not threatened by lifting the cache; the SHA itself is the natural cache key.

- **Lift the cache from `Deps` to the orchestrator scope but keep iteration-keyed lookup (one verdict per iteration, plus a "previous iteration's verdict" carry-over slot).** Rejected: still wrong axis. Iterations are an orchestration concept, not a content concept. Two iterations at the same HEAD with the same checks have the same answer; two phases of the same iteration at different HEADs (e.g. merge-time after clean-merges) have different answers. Keying on iteration loses information in both directions.

- **SHA-keyed cache as a `set[str]` of confirmed-safe SHAs (multi-slot).** Rejected: unnecessary. `git pull --ff-only` only moves HEAD forward; once the orchestrator has observed HEAD = X' and moved past X, it will never query X again. Storing past safe SHAs is dead data. The single-slot design holds the most recently observed SHA's verdict and discards earlier ones on advance — semantically equivalent for every observable behaviour, with smaller surface and no concept of "stale entries to evict."

- **SHA-keyed multi-slot cache holding both safe and broken SHAs (a `set[str]` plus `dict[str, PreflightAFK | PreflightHITL]`).** Rejected for the same reason: duplicate broken-SHA filings only matter if HEAD is ever observed twice, and `git pull --ff-only` rules that out. Single slot covering both arms (safe verdict or broken verdict) is sufficient for the deduplication case (same SHA queried twice in succession from different callers) without retaining any historical state.

- **Persist the cache across `pycastle` restarts (disk-backed safe-SHA list).** Rejected: invalidation is hard. Cache content depends on `cfg.preflight_checks` and the binary contents of every checked path at the SHA; persisting "SHA X is safe" across `pycastle` restarts means replaying every config change, every check-list edit, and every behaviour change in the check tooling. The risk of a stale persistent cache silently certifying a now-broken SHA outweighs the saving of a single re-preflight on the next launch. Process-scoped is honest: each `pycastle run` re-validates from scratch on its first `get_safe_sha()` call and earns its keep from then on.

- **Drive the preflight-fix recovery loop from inside `get_safe_sha()` (file issue → spawn Implementer + Reviewer → re-run → loop).** Rejected as out of scope and structurally wrong: the orchestrator's iteration loop already implements this loop, just at iteration granularity (a failed gate returns `PreflightAFK` → `run_iteration` routes to `implement_phase` for the filed issue → next iteration re-runs the gate). Folding the loop into `get_safe_sha()` introduces recursion (because the implement phase itself becomes a `get_safe_sha()` consumer), demands a coarse-grained lock that blocks every concurrent caller across the whole recovery run, and conflates the "ask for a SHA" question with the "drive the system through a recovery sub-orchestration" responsibility. Keeping the loop at the orchestrator layer with the cache layered underneath gives cross-iteration deduplication (single-slot returns the same cached verdict for the same broken HEAD) without any of the recursion or concurrency hazards.

- **Pull at most once per iteration (gated by a `pulled_this_iteration` flag) instead of pulling on every `get_safe_sha()` call.** Rejected: re-introduces an iteration-scoped concept the redesign is otherwise free of, and the user-visible win is small. Pulling on every call makes the contract "this returns a verdict for the most up-to-date HEAD" — a strictly more useful guarantee than "this returns a verdict for the HEAD as of iteration start." The cost is one `git pull --ff-only` per consumer call; in normal flow that is a no-op once upstream is up to date.

- **Keep `runner.preflight()` inside `agent_runner.run()` but invert `skip_preflight` to default `True`.** Rejected: cosmetic. Six callers stop writing `skip_preflight=True`, the Merger writes `skip_preflight=False`, and the asymmetry "every caller except one disables this" stays. The plumbing is the same, just spelled differently. The cleaner step is to remove the in-runner preflight altogether — the Merger's check is not "an agent prelude" but a separate question about a tree whose SHA is now part of the cache's universe.

- **Remove `runner.preflight()` and the Merger's check entirely, on the theory that the gate covers everything that matters.** Rejected: loses a real safety net. After clean-merges, HEAD's content is novel — it has never passed `PREFLIGHT_CHECKS` because no previous step held a tree with that combination of branch contents. The merge-time check exists to prevent the Merger from spending cycles on top of a baseline that quietly broke during clean integration; without it, the Merger may succeed in resolving conflicts on a broken baseline and fast-forward broken code to `main` before the next iteration's gate notices. The right move is to route the merge-time check through `get_safe_sha()` (so it benefits from the cache and shares the failure shape) rather than to delete it.

- **Have `get_safe_sha()` reuse the caller's existing sandbox (improve-sandbox, plan-sandbox, etc.) instead of opening its own ephemeral `preflight-sandbox` worktree.** Rejected: couples the cache's interface to the caller's worktree management, and creates a chicken-and-egg in the merge case (merge-sandbox is created at the SHA the call is asking the cache to validate). A self-contained `transient_worktree("preflight-sandbox", sha=sha)` lets the cache be a pure question — "is HEAD safe" — that callers ask and receive an answer to, without owning a sandbox just to ask.

- **Keep `PreflightHITL.worktree_sha` and `PreflightAFK.sha` as separately-named fields.** Rejected: `_sha_of(verdict)` plumbing in the cache exists only to paper over a naming inconsistency. Renaming `worktree_sha` to `sha` lets the verdict types share a uniform `.sha` accessor, drops the helper, and lets call sites `match` cleanly across all three arms.

- **Single-slot SHA-keyed cache, lifted to orchestrator scope, called via `get_safe_sha()`, used by every save-SHA consumer including the Merger; `skip_preflight` and `runner.preflight()` removed; `PreflightFailure` deleted; `PreflightHITL.worktree_sha` renamed `sha`.** The chosen design.

## Consequences

- A new class `PreflightCache` is introduced (likely in `iteration/preflight.py`, sibling to the existing verdict types) with one private slot `_verdict: PreflightResult | None`, one `asyncio.Lock`, and the public `async def get_safe_sha(self, deps) -> PreflightResult` method. `_PreflightDeps` keeps the same shape minus `preflight_verdict`.

- `Deps.preflight_verdict: PreflightReady | None` is removed. `Deps.preflight_cache: PreflightCache` is added. `orchestrator.run()` constructs `PreflightCache()` once before the iteration loop and threads the same instance into every `IterationDeps`, so the cache survives iteration reconstruction.

- `ensure_preflight(deps, mount_path)` is removed. The two existing callers — `improve_phase` (`improve.py:159`) and `planning_phase` (`planning.py:113`) — are rewritten to call `deps.preflight_cache.get_safe_sha(deps)`. The `mount_path` argument disappears: the cache opens its own ephemeral `transient_worktree("preflight-sandbox", sha=sha)` for the check run. Each phase's existing sandbox (improve-sandbox, plan-sandbox) is no longer the venue for `PREFLIGHT_CHECKS`; the consumer phase still creates its own sandbox afterwards via `checkout_detached` at `verdict.sha` for its agent run.

- `PlanReady.worktree_sha` is removed. The SHA no longer threads through the planner's return value into the implement phase; instead, `implement_phase` calls `deps.preflight_cache.get_safe_sha(deps)` itself at entry. The cache's single-slot design plus internal lock means the second-and-later parallel `run_issue` calls see a populated slot and return without running preflight again, so re-fetching per consumer is cheap.

- `RunRequest.skip_preflight` is removed; the per-agent preflight call at `agent_runner.py:164-167` is removed. Six call sites stop passing `skip_preflight=True` (improve phases, planner, implementer, reviewer, preflight-issue, failure-report) — the lines simply delete. The Merger (the seventh call site, the only one previously letting `skip_preflight` default to `False`) gains an explicit `await deps.preflight_cache.get_safe_sha(deps)` call inside `merge_phase`, before the `managed_worktree("merge-sandbox", ...)` block. On `PreflightReady`, the merge-sandbox is created at `verdict.sha` (the post-clean-merge HEAD that just passed) and the Merger spawns as today. On `PreflightAFK | PreflightHITL`, `merge_phase` retains today's soft-skip behaviour: log the diagnostic, skip the Merger, return `MergeResult(clean=clean_issues, conflicts=conflict_issues)` with conflicts open. The next iteration's first `get_safe_sha()` call observes the same broken HEAD, returns the same cached verdict (no duplicate issue filed), and the orchestrator's preflight-fix path routes the existing issue through `implement_phase`.

- `PreflightFailure` is deleted from `errors.py`. The class has no remaining raise site once `runner.preflight()` is removed. ADR 0013's rationale (`PreflightFailure` as exception, not value-result) is superseded by removal: there is no longer an asymmetry between exception-style and return-style error reporting in `agent_runner.run()` because preflight is no longer one of `agent_runner.run()`'s concerns. The merge-time `try/except PreflightFailure` block in `merge.py` is replaced with the verdict pattern-match described above.

- `PreflightHITL.worktree_sha` is renamed `sha`. All three verdict types (`PreflightReady`, `PreflightAFK`, `PreflightHITL`) now expose a uniform `.sha` field. Call sites that previously read `result.worktree_sha` switch to `result.sha`. The naming alignment is independent of the cache redesign but lands in the same change because the new verdict pattern-match in the cache and in the merge-time call site reads cleaner over a uniform field.

- Pull-on-every-call means `_wait_for_clean_working_tree` and `git_svc.pull()` run inside every `get_safe_sha()` invocation, gated by the cache's lock. In normal flow (upstream not advancing) this is a no-op pull plus a cache hit on the SHA; the wall-clock cost is small. The lock serialises all callers, including parallel `run_issue` instances, so in implement phase only one caller pays the pull cost and the rest see a populated slot. The lock must be constructed on a running event loop's terms — `PreflightCache()` is constructed inside `orchestrator.run()` (which is already an async context) so the lock binds to the same loop the iterations use; pycastle currently runs a single event loop for the whole process invocation, and the cache assumes that invariant.

- The CONTEXT.md term **safe SHA** changes definition: "the SHA most recently observed at HEAD by `PreflightCache.get_safe_sha()` and confirmed by `PREFLIGHT_CHECKS` in an ephemeral `preflight-sandbox`; cached as a single-slot verdict that survives across iterations until HEAD advances." The terms **preflight gate** and **preflight pull** collapse into the cache's contract; **merge-time preflight skip** stays as a behaviour but its mechanism becomes "soft-skip on `PreflightAFK | PreflightHITL` from `get_safe_sha()` inside `merge_phase`" rather than "catch `PreflightFailure` from `agent_runner.run()`."

- Tests that injected `Deps.preflight_verdict` to bypass preflight (e.g. `test_preflight.py:34`, `test_planning.py:277`, `test_iteration.py:362`, `test_orchestrator.py:1953/1994`) migrate to seeding `PreflightCache._verdict` (or, if the test prefers black-box stubbing, supplying a `PreflightCache` subclass whose `get_safe_sha()` returns a fixture verdict). Tests that asserted on `PlanReady.worktree_sha` adapt to the new return shape; tests that exercised `runner.preflight()` indirectly via `skip_preflight=False` test paths (only `test_merge.py` and `test_agent_runner.py`) switch to asserting on the merge-time `get_safe_sha()` call and the absence of any `runner.preflight()` invocation.

- Slicing: the change naturally splits into three commits — (1) introduce `PreflightCache`, lift verdict cache from `Deps` to the new class, rewire improve and planning, drop `PlanReady.worktree_sha`, rename `worktree_sha` → `sha`; (2) make `implement_phase` self-fetch and drop SHA threading through `run_iteration` → `_run_implement_and_merge` → `implement_phase` → `run_issue`; (3) remove `RunRequest.skip_preflight` and `runner.preflight()`, move the Merger's check to `merge_phase` via `get_safe_sha()`, delete `PreflightFailure`. Slices land in order; (1) and (2) compose, (3) is independent and the most invasive (touches `agent_runner.py`, every `RunRequest` site, `merge.py`, and `errors.py`).
